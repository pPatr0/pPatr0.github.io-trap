About Trapholt

Trapholt is a museum for modern art, handicrafts and design with unique architecture and a scenic location down to the Kolding fjord.

Trapholt is a national cultural beacon in the design city of Kolding, which is a member of UNESCO's Creative Cities Network.

The museum's architecture is an attraction in itself. Built around the central museum street, the exhibition rooms open up exciting spatial experiences. Outside stands the strong, sculptural wall that delimits the museum to a large sculpture park with works by Danish contemporary artists.

Participation is absolutely central at Trapholt. The museum shows new paths both nationally and internationally through sensory exhibitions, involving projects and research that expands the framework for what is understood by an art museum, what experiences you can expect and new forms of interaction.

Trapholt offers changing exhibitions every year and has, among other things, Denmark's largest chair collection of more than 500 chairs from the 20th century. Trapholt establishes inclusive projects in collaboration with artists, where citizens are invited to participate with concrete creative contributions or personal resources that can create change.

Arne Jacobsen's Kubeflex summer house is a total experience for the audience interested in design and architecture; the only one of its kind and furnished with Jacobsen's own designs. At the museum, a special room is dedicated to paintings by Richard Mortensen – a room that has been created in close collaboration with the artist himself.

In Café Gustav Lind you can enjoy the view over Kolding Fjord and in Trapholt Design shop a wide selection of interior design from Danish and international brands is presented.

There is free parking right at the door for cars and buses. Trapholt is handicap-friendly.
